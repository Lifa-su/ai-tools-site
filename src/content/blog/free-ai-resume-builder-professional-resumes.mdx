## How ATS Systems Work (And Why Most Resumes Fail)

Before diving into the how-to, it's worth understanding what you're optimizing for. ATS software parses your resume into structured data fields: name, contact info, work experience, education, skills. It then scores your application based on how well it matches the job description.

Here's where most resumes fail:

### Formatting Issues
ATS systems struggle with complex layouts. Multi-column designs, text boxes, headers/footers, and embedded images can cause parsing errors. Your beautifully designed resume might look like gibberish to the software.

### Missing Keywords
If the job posting asks for "project management" and your resume says "managed projects," some ATS tools won't make the connection. AI resume builders analyze job descriptions and mirror the exact terminology.

### Inconsistent Structure
ATS expects standard sections: Summary, Experience, Education, Skills. Creative section titles like "My Journey" or "What I Bring" can confuse parsers and cause your information to be categorized incorrectly.

### Wrong File Format
Some systems handle PDFs well; others prefer .docx. A good AI resume generator outputs in ATS-compatible formats and ensures clean parsing regardless of the system.
